Together against breast cancer

Why Pink October?

Every year, Pink October is dedicated to raising awareness about breast cancer and the importance of early detection. It is a global movement to support patients, survivors, and encourage research.

How to Protect Yourself?

  • Get regular mammograms after the age of 40, or earlier if recommended.
  • Consult your doctor if you notice any unusual changes.
  • Adopt a balanced diet rich in fruits and vegetables.
  • Exercise regularly and maintain a healthy weight.
  • Avoid smoking and limit alcohol consumption.

Signs & Symptoms

Be aware of changes in your body. Common warning signs include:

  • Lumps or thickening in the breast or underarm.
  • Change in the size or shape of the breast.
  • Unusual nipple discharge or inversion.
  • Redness, swelling, or skin dimpling on the breast.

Risk Factors

Some factors increase the risk of developing breast cancer:

  • Family history of breast cancer.
  • Genetic mutations (BRCA1 and BRCA2 genes).
  • Hormonal replacement therapy.
  • Lack of physical activity.

Early Detection Saves Lives

When breast cancer is detected at an early stage, the chances of successful treatment are significantly higher. Self-exams and regular check-ups play a key role in prevention.
